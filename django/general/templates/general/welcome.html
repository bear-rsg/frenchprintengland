Welcome

French-language books printed in England from 1480 to 1685 are an essential corpus for understanding how cross-Channel cultural mobility informed early modern writing and reading practices. They offer valuable insights into religious, political, social, and cultural life of the time. Yet they are little known because they fall outside the boundaries of English and French Literature traditionally conceived.

Our project, funded by the Leverhulme Trust and based at the Universities of Birmingham and Cambridge, is the first detailed study of these texts. You can use this website to learn about our team and objectives, discover more about how we are compiling our corpus, access our project database, and download our project publications.

This website has been developed for the French-Language Print Publications in England to 1685
research project led by Dr Peter Auger at the University of Birmingham

The project is funded by a Leverhulme Trust grant
